# Battle of Britain

## Overview
The Battle of Britain (July - October 1940) was the first major military defeat of Nazi Germany. It prevented Britain from being invaded and demonstrated that the Luftwaffe could be beaten.

## German Strategy
After defeating France, Hitler sought to force Britain to negotiate or surrender. The Luftwaffe was tasked with destroying the Royal Air Force (RAF) to prepare for Operation Sea Lion, the planned invasion of Britain.

## The Air War
Germany launched heavy bombing raids against British airfields, radar stations, and cities. The RAF, outnumbered but using radar effectively and flying superior tactics, inflicted heavy losses on the Luftwaffe.

## British Resilience
British pilots, many of whom were volunteers from occupied countries, fought valiantly. Prime Minister Winston Churchill's speeches inspired national resistance. The phrase "Never was so much owed by so many to so few" honored the pilots.

## Outcome
By October 1940, the Luftwaffe had failed to achieve air superiority. Hitler postponed and eventually cancelled Operation Sea Lion. Britain remained in the war as a base for future Allied operations, including D-Day.
