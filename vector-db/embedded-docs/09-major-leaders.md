# Major Leaders of World War II

## Allied Leaders

### Winston Churchill (Britain)
Prime Minister Winston Churchill led Britain through its darkest hours. His defiant speeches and steady leadership maintained British morale. He maintained the "Special Relationship" with the US and coordinated Allied strategy.

### Franklin D. Roosevelt (USA)
President Roosevelt guided America from isolation to global involvement. He developed the "Arsenal of Democracy" and oversaw the massive production effort. He met with Churchill and Stalin at key conferences to plan Allied strategy.

### Joseph Stalin (Soviet Union)
Stalin led the Soviet Union through the war on the Eastern Front. Soviet forces bore the brunt of fighting Germany. Stalin was a ruthless leader but essential to Allied victory. He attended key wartime conferences.

## Axis Leaders

### Adolf Hitler (Germany)
Führer of Nazi Germany, Hitler directed German military strategy. His decisions, such as declaring war on the US and refusing to allow retreats, contributed to defeat. He committed suicide in April 1945 as Soviet forces approached Berlin.

### Benito Mussolini (Italy)
Duce of Italy, Mussolini led Italy into war alongside Germany. Italian military failures forced German intervention. Mussolini was overthrown in 1943 and later executed by Italian partisans.

### Emperor Hirohito (Japan)
Emperor Hirohito was Japan's head of state during the war. His role in decision-making remains debated. He announced Japan's surrender in August 1945, preserving the imperial institution.
