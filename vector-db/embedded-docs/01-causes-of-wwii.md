# Causes of World War II

## Treaty of Versailles (1919)
The Treaty of Versailles formally ended World War I but imposed harsh penalties on Germany. Germany lost territory, faced massive reparations, and had military restrictions. This created economic hardship and national humiliation that fueled German resentment.

## Rise of Fascism
The Great Depression devastated economies worldwide. Political instability led to the rise of fascist movements in Germany, Italy, and Japan. Adolf Hitler in Germany, Benito Mussolini in Italy, and militarists in Japan seized power, promoting aggressive nationalism and expansionism.

## German Expansion
Hitler violated the Treaty of Versailles by remilitarizing the Rhineland (1936), annexing Austria (1938), and taking the Sudetenland from Czechoslovakia (1938). The appeasement policy of Britain and France failed to stop German aggression.

## Japanese Aggression in Asia
Japan invaded Manchuria in 1931 and China in 1937, seeking resources and territory. The League of Nations failed to stop Japanese expansion, emboldening other Axis powers.

## Failure of Diplomacy
The League of Nations proved ineffective in maintaining peace. Diplomatic failures and the Munich Agreement (1938) that allowed German occupation of Czechoslovakia demonstrated the weakness of collective security.
