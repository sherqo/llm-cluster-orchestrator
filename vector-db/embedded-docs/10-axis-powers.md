# The Axis Powers

## Germany
Nazi Germany was the dominant Axis power. Under Adolf Hitler, Germany conquered much of Europe through blitzkrieg warfare. The Wehrmacht (German armed forces) fought on multiple fronts. Germany developed advanced weapons including jet aircraft and rockets. Nazi ideology drove the Holocaust and other war crimes. Germany's defeat in 1945 ended the Axis alliance.

## Italy
Italy, led by Benito Mussolini, joined Germany in 1940. Italian military performance was generally poor, requiring German assistance in North Africa, Greece, and the Soviet Union. Italy switched sides in 1943 after Allied invasion, with the Italian Co-Belligerent Army fighting alongside the Allies. Italian forces also fought in Ethiopia and the Balkans.

## Japan
Japan, governed by militarists and Emperor Hirohito, pursued expansion in Asia and the Pacific. Japanese forces conquered much of China, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific islands. The Japanese military used kamikaze attacks and committed war crimes. Japan's surrender after atomic bombs ended the war.

## Minor Axis Partners
Other nations joined the Axis: Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, and collaborationist governments in occupied territories. These countries contributed forces and resources to the war effort, though none matched the major powers.
