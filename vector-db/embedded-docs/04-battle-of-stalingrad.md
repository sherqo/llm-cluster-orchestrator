# Battle of Stalingrad

## Overview
The Battle of Stalingrad (August 1942 - February 1943) was the largest and bloodiest battle of World War II. It marked a major turning point in the war on the Eastern Front.

## The German Advance
German forces advanced into southern Russia in summer 1942. Army Group South targeted the oil fields of the Caucasus and the city of Stalingrad on the Volga River. The Wehrmacht reached Stalingrad in September.

## Urban Combat
Fighting devolved into brutal street-by-street, house-by-house combat. Both sides suffered enormous casualties in brutal urban warfare. Soviet defenders, commanded by Marshal Georgy Zhukov, were ordered to hold the city at all costs.

## Soviet Counteroffensive
In November 1942, Soviet forces launched Operation Uranus, encircling the German Sixth Army. German attempts to break the encirclement failed. Hitler refused to allow retreat.

## Surrender and Impact
On February 2, 1943, the remaining 91,000 German troops surrendered. Only 5,000 would survive captivity. The battle ended Germany's advance into the Soviet Union and initiated a sustained Soviet counteroffensive that would reach Berlin.
