# Atomic Bombs on Japan

## Development
The Manhattan Project, initiated in 1942, developed the atomic bomb. Scientists including Einstein, Oppenheimer, and Fermi created the world's first nuclear weapons in secret laboratories in Los Alamos, New Mexico.

## Hiroshima
On August 6, 1945, the US dropped "Little Boy," a uranium gun-type bomb, on Hiroshima, Japan. The blast killed approximately 80,000 people immediately, with tens of thousands more dying from radiation in following weeks. The city was largely destroyed.

## Nagasaki
On August 9, 1945, the US dropped "Fat Man," a plutonium implosion bomb, on Nagasaki. Approximately 40,000 died immediately. The bomb destroyed much of the city. Japan had not yet surrendered despite the devastation.

## Japanese Surrender
On August 15, 1945, Emperor Hirohito announced Japan's surrender. The following day, World War II ended. The surrender formally came on September 2, 1945, aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay.

## Debate and Legacy
The bombs' use remains debated. Some argue they forced Japanese surrender and prevented Allied invasion casualties. Others argue Japan was already near surrender. The bombs ushered in the nuclear age and shaped Cold War geopolitics.
