# Pacific Theater

## Overview
The Pacific Theater was the war between Japan and the Allied powers, primarily the United States. It spanned the vast Pacific Ocean, featuring island hopping, naval battles, and aerial warfare.

## Japanese Expansion
Japan conquered territories from Burma to the Central Pacific, including the Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore, and numerous islands. The Japanese Navy and Army projected power across the region.

## Key Battles
Major Pacific battles included: Coral Sea (May 1942) - first carrier vs. carrier battle; Midway (June 1942) - turning point where US sank four Japanese carriers; Guadalcanal (1942-43) - first major Allied offensive; Iwo Jima and Okinawa (1945) - brutal island assaults approaching Japan.

## Island Hopping
Allied strategy was to "hop" over heavily defended islands to capture those with strategic value. This bypassed Japanese strongholds while building bases for bomber attacks on Japan. The US Navy's islander carriers dominated the war.

## Naval Warfare
The Pacific war was defined by aircraft carriers. The US developed naval superiority through superior industrial capacity and tactical innovation. Submarine warfare devastated Japanese shipping.

## End of Pacific War
The Pacific War ended with Japan's surrender after atomic bombs. The war in the Pacific was characterized by fierce combat, Japanese kamikaze attacks, and the first use of nuclear weapons.
