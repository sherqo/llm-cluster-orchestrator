# Attack on Pearl Harbor

## Date and Overview
On December 7, 1941, Japan launched a surprise military attack on the United States naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. The attack lasted about two hours and fundamentally changed the course of World War II.

## The Attack
Japanese carrier-based aircraft launched in two waves, totaling 353 planes. They targeted battleships, aircraft, and military installations. The attack killed 2,403 Americans and wounded 1,178. Eight battleships were damaged or destroyed.

## Strategic Goals
Japan sought to destroy the US Pacific Fleet to prevent American interference with Japanese expansion in Southeast Asia. Japanese leaders believed a decisive victory would force America to negotiate.

## Immediate Aftermath
President Franklin D. Roosevelt called December 8 "a date which will live in infamy." Congress declared war on Japan the same day. Germany and Italy declared war on the United States on December 11, bringing America fully into the global conflict.

## Significance
The attack united American public opinion behind the war effort. The US shifted from isolationism to full involvement, becoming the "Arsenal of Democracy" and ultimately contributing Allied victory.
