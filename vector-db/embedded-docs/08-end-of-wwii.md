# End of World War II

## European Victory
Germany surrendered unconditionally on May 7, 1945, ending the war in Europe. Victory in Europe Day (V-E Day) was celebrated on May 8. Nazi leadership was arrested, and Allied forces occupied Germany.

## Pacific War Continues
Despite Germany's defeat, the war continued in the Pacific. Japan refused Allied demands for unconditional surrender. The US dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9).

## Japanese Surrender
On August 15, 1945, Emperor Hirohito announced Japan's surrender in a radio address. This was the first time many Japanese heard their emperor's voice. The formal surrender ceremony took place on September 2, 1945.

## Victory over Japan Day
V-J Day, August 15, 1945, marked the end of World War II. The war had lasted nearly six years and killed approximately 70-85 million people, making it the deadliest conflict in human history.

## Consequences
The war redrew the map of Europe and decolonized Asia. The United Nations was established. The US and Soviet Union emerged as superpowers, beginning the Cold War. The war's legacy shaped international relations for decades.
