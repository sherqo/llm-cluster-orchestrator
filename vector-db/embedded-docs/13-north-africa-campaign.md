# North Africa Campaign

## Overview
The North Africa campaign (1940-1943) was fought in Libya, Egypt, Tunisia, and Morocco. Allied and Axis forces battled for control of the region and its oil resources and shipping routes.

## Early German Success
Italian forces initially struggled against British forces in Egypt. German reinforcements under Erwin Rommel (the "Desert Fox") arrived in 1941 and pushed British forces back toward Egypt. The "Africa Korps" became renowned.

## El Alamein
The Battle of El Alamein (October-November 1942) was the turning point. British forces under Bernard Montgomery broke through Axis lines. Rommel retreated, unable to hold positions without reinforcements.

## Operation Torch
Allied forces landed in Morocco and Algeria (Operation Torch, November 1942). Combined with British advances from Egypt, Axis forces in North Africa were trapped. By May 1943, over 275,000 Axis troops surrendered.

## Significance
North Africa was the first major Allied theater victory. It demonstrated Allied cooperation and prepared forces for the invasion of Italy. The campaign also showed the importance of logistics and supply lines in desert warfare.
