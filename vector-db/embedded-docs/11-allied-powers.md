# The Allied Powers

## The Big Three
The main Allied powers were the United States, Soviet Union, and United Kingdom. These "Big Three" leaders met at conferences to coordinate strategy: Tehran (1943), Yalta (1945), and Potsdam (1945). Despite tensions, they agreed on defeating Germany and Japan.

## United States
The US became the "Arsenal of Democracy," producing massive amounts of weapons, vehicles, and supplies. American forces fought in Europe after D-Day and across the Pacific. The US developed and used atomic bombs. Lend-Lease provided vital aid to Allies.

## Soviet Union
The Soviet Union suffered the most casualties, fighting Germany on the Eastern Front. Soviet forces captured Berlin and liberated Eastern Europe. Soviet contributions were essential to Allied victory despite being underrepresented in Western narratives.

## United Kingdom
Britain stood alone against Germany after France's fall in 1940. The Royal Air Force won the Battle of Britain. British forces fought in North Africa, Italy, and at D-Day. British intelligence and codebreaking contributed significantly.

## Other Allies
China fought Japan since 1937. France liberated itself in 1944. Other nations including Canada, Australia, India, and many others contributed forces and resources. The United Nations was formed from the Allied coalition.
