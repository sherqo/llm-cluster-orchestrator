# The Holocaust

## Overview
The Holocaust was the systematic, state-sponsored persecution and murder of six million Jews by the Nazi regime and its collaborators between 1933 and 1945.

## Nazi Persecution
Nazi ideology viewed Jews as inferior "subhumans." Beginning in 1933, Jews faced discriminatory laws, property confiscation, and violence. The Nuremberg Laws (1935) stripped Jews of citizenship and legal rights.

## Ghettos and Camps
Jews were forced into overcrowded ghettos in occupied Poland. Beginning in 1941, Nazi Germany established extermination camps where victims were killed in gas chambers. Other camps used forced labor and starvation.

## "Final Solution"
In January 1942, Nazi leaders met at the Wannsee Conference to coordinate the "Final Solution to the Jewish Question." This was the systematic murder of European Jews. Approximately six million Jews died, along with millions of others including Roma, disabled people, and political prisoners.

## Liberation and Testimony
Allied forces liberated concentration camps in 1944-1945, revealing the horrors to the world. Survivors testified at the Nuremberg Trials. The Holocaust became a defining event in world history, leading to the phrase "Never Again."
