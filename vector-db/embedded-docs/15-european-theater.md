# European Theater

## Overview
The European Theater was the war in Europe between the Allies and Axis powers. It was fought across continental Europe, from the Atlantic to the Eastern Front, and involved the largest armies and most brutal fighting.

## German Conquests
Germany quickly conquered Poland (1939), Denmark and Norway (1940), and France (1940). The blitzkrieg strategy overwhelmed smaller nations. Britain resisted while the Soviet Union was initially allied with Germany via non-aggression pact.

## Eastern Front
The Eastern Front was the largest and deadliest theater. Germany invaded the Soviet Union in 1941 (Operation Barbarossa). Battles like Stalingrad and Kursk were among the largest in history. The Soviets pushed Germany west to Berlin.

## Western Front
The Western Front was relatively quiet after 1940 until D-Day (June 1944). Allied forces advanced from Normandy into Germany, meeting Soviet forces in 1945. The Western front included the Battle of the Bulge (1944-45), Germany's last major offensive.

## Italy Campaign
Allies invaded Italy in 1943 after defeating North Africa. Italian surrender led to German resistance. The campaign was difficult, featuring mountain warfare and the Gothic Line. Italy liberated in April 1945.

## End of War
Germany surrendered in May 1945. The European Theater ended with Hitler's death, Germany's unconditional surrender, and the division of Europe between Western and Soviet spheres.
